## One Core Rule (Read This First)

> **Context must be reviewed before performance.**

Severity, intake mix, and hospital role **shape every downstream metric**.  
Skipping context pages leads to incorrect conclusions.

## How to Navigate the Pages (In Order)

### 1ï¸âƒ£ Severity Mix (Patient Complexity)
**Question:** Are patients sicker here?

- Shows how complex the patient population is
- Higher severity explains higher cost, longer stays, and higher risk
- This page provides **context only**, not performance

> Do not interpret any outcome or cost without this page.

---

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Unplanned Admissions (Intake Pressure)
**Question:** Is pressure driven by emergency intake?

- High unplanned intake strains beds, staff, and flow
- High rates are expected in trauma, referral, and safety-net hospitals
- This explains downstream congestion

> Read before Length of Stay or Cost.

---

### 3ï¸âƒ£ Length of Stay (Throughput)
**Question:** Are patients staying longer than expected?

- Longer stays increase cost and reduce capacity
- LOS must rise with patient severity
- Flat LOS across severity levels is a warning sign

> LOS without severity context is misleading.

---

### 4ï¸âƒ£ Disposition Outcomes (Where Patients Go)
**Question:** What happens after discharge?

- Home vs post-acute care vs transfers vs death
- Higher post-acute use often reflects patient complexity
- Explains downstream cost and capacity impact

---

### 5ï¸âƒ£ Mortality (Outcome Risk)
**Question:** Do death rates align with patient risk?

- Mortality is rare but high-impact
- Always shown with encounter counts
- Small hospitals may show volatile rates

> Rates without volume are unreliable.

---

### 6ï¸âƒ£ Cost & Margin Pressure (Financial Stress)
**Question:** Is care financially sustainable?

- Shows cost per patient and cost vs charges
- Indicates **financial pressure**, not profitability
- Must be interpreted with LOS and severity

---

### 7ï¸âƒ£ Payer Mix (Structural Financial Risk)
**Question:** Is pressure operational or reimbursement-driven?

- Shows who pays for care
- High Medicaid or self-pay share reflects mission, not inefficiency
- Explains structural financial strain

## How to Use Comparisons Correctly

- Hospitals are compared **only within peer groups**
- Differences across hospital roles are expected
- Large differences *within* a peer group are meaningful

> Peer context is mandatory for fair interpretation.
